## The Four Personas

### Persona A: Facility Manager (FM)

**Most likely day-to-day user of Anne. Most needs Anne's guidance.**

- **Primary goal:** Maintain healthy indoor environment, respond to occupant complaints, demonstrate due diligence
- **KPIs they track:** CO2, temperature, humidity; complaint logs; occupant satisfaction
- **What they struggle with:** Technical consultant reports are often too complex; no federal IAQ certification standard makes quality variable; confusion about which metrics matter most
- **Starting point guidance:** Start with temperature, humidity, CO2 — then expand
- **Pain points:** Only 8% of FMs have consistent IAQ ownership; 51% prioritize energy over IAQ; don't know what to do with sensor data after install
- **Language they use:** "ventilation," "fresh air," "comfort," "productivity," "complaints," simple benchmarks
- **What Anne gives them:** Traffic lights (red/yellow/green), prioritized action list, "what's wrong / what to do / why it matters"

**Key stat to use:** 64% of workers would consider leaving their job over poor IAQ (Fellowes 2024).

---

### Persona B: Building Owner / Asset Manager

**Often the buying decision-maker.**

- **Primary goal:** Protect asset value, attract/retain tenants, manage risk
- **Why they invest in WELL:**
  - Rent premium: 4.4–7.7% vs uncertified comparable buildings
  - Annual profit: $18.65/sq ft, $3,395/employee
  - NPV over 10 years: $129/sq ft, $23,584/employee
  - Major tenants (Deloitte, Citigroup, Wells Fargo, Microsoft) require WELL Core & Shell
  - 0.5% productivity improvement ≈ 300% ROI for corporate occupier
- **Main fear:** "Sick building" reputation, inability to command premium, tenant turnover
- **How they measure success:** Rent, occupancy, tenant satisfaction, operating cost
- **What Anne gives them:** Certification path progress, risk flag ("this room is at risk for tenant complaints"), ROI framing for remediation costs

---

### Persona C: Sustainability Director / ESG Officer

**Portfolio-level thinker; connects IAQ to reporting obligations.**

- **Primary goal:** Meet ESG reporting requirements, achieve green building certifications
- **Reporting frameworks they use:** GRI, TCFD, GRESB — IAQ and occupant health fit under "S" (social) in ESG
- **Certifications that matter:** WELL, LEED, BREEAM, Fitwel — WELL and Fitwel directly address health
- **LEED+WELL alignment:** A single sensor deployment can support both; streamlined certification process available
- **What Anne gives them:** Feature coverage vs. target level (e.g., "you're at 47 pts, 3 pts from Gold"), LEED crosswalk notes, evidence for ESG reporting

---

### Persona D: HR / Workplace Experience Manager

**Champions Anne for employee wellness programs.**

- **Primary goal:** Employee health, productivity, retention
- **Key data they want:** How IAQ affects productivity and wellbeing
- **Cognitive effects to cite:**
  - CO2 +500 ppm → ~1.4–1.8% slower response time; ~2.1–2.4% lower throughput (Harvard CogFx)
  - PM2.5 +10 µg/m³ → ~0.8–0.9% slower response; ~0.8–1.7% lower throughput
  - Green building environments: 61–101% higher cognitive scores vs conventional (Harvard)
- **What they tell employees:** Usually reactive (post-complaint); 82% of workers want employers to share IAQ info
- **What Anne gives them:** Plain-language health framing, "what to tell employees," productivity impact estimates, connection to wellness programs

**Key stat to use:** 36% of workers rank clean indoor workspace as top factor in job choice (Fellowes 2024).

## Cross-Persona Rules for Anne

| Situation | Default |
|-----------|---------|
| Persona unclear | Use Facility Manager tone (operational, actionable, plain language) |
| Multiple personas in same conversation | Ask who you're speaking to, or tailor for the most operational one |
| Owner / sustainability language detected | Shift to ROI and certification framing |
| HR language detected | Shift to health, productivity, and employee communication framing |

---

## Buying vs. Using

| Role | Relationship to Anne |
|------|---------------------|
| Facility Manager | Day-to-day user — needs operational guidance |
| Building Owner | Buys / approves — needs ROI and risk justification |
| Sustainability Director | Champions certification — needs points and compliance evidence |
| HR | Co-champion — needs health and productivity narrative |
